Mini Case Spots

READ & REFLECT  |  NO SUBMISSION REQUIRED

Purpose: AI is not limited to one field. It shows up in healthcare, retail, logistics, and education. Each application uses a particular AI workload, which is the type of task the AI system is designed to perform.

What to do: Read each short scenario below. After each one, notice the AI Workload Tag that identifies the main type of AI at work. At the end, think through the reflection questions and reveal a sample answer to compare your thinking.

CASE SCENARIOS

Case 1  |  Clinic Triage Support

A community health clinic uses a digital intake tool that asks patients a series of questions about their symptoms when they check in. The system reads the patient's typed responses and assigns a priority level (urgent, soon, or routine) before a nurse ever sees the chart. It also flags phrases like "chest tightness" or "shortness of breath" for immediate review, even if the patient did not mark the visit as an emergency.

🏷 AI Workload
Natural Language Processing (NLP) + Classification

Case 2  |  Retail Demand Forecasting

A regional grocery chain uses a forecasting system that analyzes the last three years of sales data, local weather patterns, and upcoming holidays to predict how much of each product to stock the following week. If a snowstorm is expected, the system automatically increases orders for bread, milk, and batteries. Store managers can override recommendations, but the system learns from those corrections over time.

🏷 AI Workload
Predictive Analytics / Machine Learning (Regression)

Case 3  |  Warehouse Routing

Inside a large fulfillment center, dozens of mobile robots navigate a grid of shelving to retrieve items for outgoing orders. A central AI system assigns each robot a route in real time, continuously recalculating paths to avoid collisions, account for congestion, and minimize the total distance traveled. When a robot's battery drops below a threshold, the system reroutes it to a charging station while automatically reassigning its task to another robot.

🏷 AI Workload
Optimization / Reinforcement Learning

Case 4  |  Campus Chatbot

A community college launched a virtual assistant available 24/7 on its website. Students ask questions like "When is the last day to withdraw?" or "Where do I go for financial aid?" and receive instant, personalized answers. The chatbot is trained on the college catalog, academic calendar, and student handbook. When it cannot answer a question with confidence, it offers to connect the student with a live advisor during business hours.

🏷 AI Workload
Conversational AI / Natural Language Processing (NLP)

REFLECTION QUESTIONS

Take a moment to think through each question on your own, then click the button to reveal a sample answer and compare your thinking.

1. Look at the four AI workload tags. Which two seem most similar to you, and what is one key difference between them?

Sample Answer
NLP + Classification and Conversational AI / NLP are the most similar since both involve understanding human language. The key difference is purpose: classification assigns a fixed category or priority (such as urgent vs. routine), while conversational AI is designed to hold a back-and-forth exchange and respond to open-ended questions.

2. Choose one scenario and think of a different industry where the same workload type could be useful. Briefly explain why.

Sample Answer
Predictive analytics (like the grocery forecasting case) could be very useful in education. A school district could analyze past enrollment data, local economic trends, and demographic shifts to predict how many students will enroll each semester, helping administrators plan staffing and allocate resources more accurately.

3. All four cases involve AI making decisions or suggestions. In which case do you think it is most important for a human to stay in the loop? Why?

Sample Answer
The clinic triage case is where human oversight matters most. A misclassification could mean a seriously ill patient is not seen quickly enough. Unlike a routing mistake or a stock shortage, a triage error could directly harm someone's health, so a nurse or clinician should always review and be able to override the system's recommendation.
